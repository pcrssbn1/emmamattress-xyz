About Emma Mattress: Our Story and Approach to Better Sleep

Company Origins and International Expansion

Emma mattress began in Frankfurt, Germany in 2015 when co-founders Dennis Schmoltzi and Manuel Mueller identified an opportunity to disrupt the traditional mattress retail model through direct-to-consumer sales. The company name "Emma" was chosen for its universal recognition across European languages and its association with comfort and home. Within two years of launch, Emma became the best-selling mattress brand in Germany, achieving this position through aggressive online marketing, generous trial periods, and competitive pricing that undercut traditional furniture stores by 40% to 60%.

The company's expansion strategy focused first on European markets, entering the United Kingdom, France, and Ireland between 2016 and 2017. By 2018, Emma operated in 18 countries across Europe and had begun manufacturing partnerships in Asia to support global growth. The United States market entry came in 2018, though Emma faced significantly more competition from established bed-in-a-box brands like Casper, Purple, and Tuft & Needle, which had already captured substantial market share. To differentiate itself in the crowded US market, Emma emphasized its 365-night trial period, the longest in the industry at that time, and positioned its products as European-engineered alternatives to American brands.

Emma's parent company, Emma Sleep GmbH, raised over $150 million in venture capital funding between 2017 and 2020, enabling rapid expansion and product line diversification. In 2021, Emma merged with Haniel, a German investment firm, in a deal reportedly valued at approximately €200 million. This acquisition provided additional resources for research and development while maintaining the Emma brand identity and operational independence. Today, Emma employs over 600 people globally and generates estimated annual revenue exceeding $400 million across all markets, making it one of the largest mattress companies operating exclusively through direct-to-consumer channels.

Product Development Philosophy and Testing Standards

Emma mattress development follows a testing protocol that involves sleep studies with diverse participant groups representing different body types, sleep positions, and age ranges. The company maintains a sleep laboratory in Germany where prototype mattresses undergo evaluation for pressure distribution, temperature regulation, and durability. Each mattress design goes through at least 12 months of testing before commercial release, with participants sleeping on prototypes for minimum 30-night periods and providing feedback through daily sleep journals and periodic interviews.

The foam materials used in Emma mattresses are CertiPUR-US certified in the United States, meaning they meet standards for content, emissions, and durability established by independent laboratories. This certification ensures the foams contain no prohibited phthalates, no heavy metals, no formaldehyde, and no ozone depleters. The foams must also meet low VOC emissions standards, with total volatile organic compound emissions below 0.5 parts per million. Emma sources its memory foam from suppliers in both the United States and Europe, with manufacturing facilities located strategically to reduce shipping distances and environmental impact.

Quality control procedures include compression testing that simulates 10 years of use by applying 100,000 compression cycles to mattress samples. Emma's warranty covers defects including sagging greater than 1.5 inches, which research indicates affects sleep quality and spinal alignment. The company also conducts regular third-party testing through organizations like OEKO-TEX Standard 100, which certifies that textile components meet human-ecological requirements and contain no harmful substances. Customer Service Approach and Sustainability Initiatives

Emma mattress operates customer service centers in each major market, with US-based support available via phone, email, and live chat during extended hours Monday through Saturday. The company's return process for trial period returns involves scheduling a free pickup rather than requiring customers to repackage and ship the mattress themselves. Emma partners with local charities and donation centers to redirect returned mattresses to families in need, homeless shelters, and disaster relief organizations. According to company data, approximately 8% to 12% of Emma mattresses sold are returned during the trial period, which aligns with industry averages for online mattress retailers.

Sustainability initiatives include mattress recycling programs in markets where infrastructure supports foam and textile separation. In the United States, Emma works with the Mattress Recycling Council, a nonprofit organization operating in California, Connecticut, and Rhode Island where mattress recycling is mandated by state law. These programs recover steel, foam, and fiber components for reuse in carpet padding, insulation, and other applications. Emma's packaging uses recycled cardboard and has been redesigned multiple times to reduce material use, with current packaging weighing 40% less than the original 2015 design while maintaining protection during shipping.

The company has committed to carbon-neutral shipping by 2025 through a combination of logistics optimization and carbon offset purchases supporting reforestation projects. Emma's website includes a sustainability page detailing these initiatives, though critics note that mattress manufacturing remains resource-intensive regardless of end-of-life recycling efforts.
